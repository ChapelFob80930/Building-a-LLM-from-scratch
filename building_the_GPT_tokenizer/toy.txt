Kanye West

Kanye Omari West (now legally Ye) is an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, fashion designer, and entrepreneur. He was born on June 8, 1977, in Atlanta, Georgia, and raised in Chicago, Illinois. He first became famous as a producer for Roc-A-Fella Records, especially through his work with Jay-Z, before becoming one of the most influential hip-hop artists of all time. His production style, especially early in his career, became known for sped-up soul samples often referred to as “chipmunk soul.”

West dropped out of college to pursue music full time, which inspired the title of his debut album The College Dropout (2004). The album was critically acclaimed and commercially successful. He followed it with Late Registration (2005), Graduation (2007), and 808s & Heartbreak (2008). 808s & Heartbreak heavily influenced modern melodic rap and artists who mixed singing with rap.

His 2010 album My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy is widely regarded as one of the greatest albums of the 2010s. Yeezus (2013) became the most critically acclaimed album of 2013 according to Metacritic. The Life of Pablo (2016) became the first album to top the Billboard 200 while going platinum in the United States and gold in the UK through streaming alone.

West has won 24 Grammy Awards from 75 nominations. In 2008 he became the first solo artist whose first three albums were all nominated for Album of the Year at the Grammys. He also received awards such as the Billboard Artist Achievement Award and the Michael Jackson Video Vanguard Award.

His first six solo albums were included in Rolling Stone’s 2020 list of the 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. Entertainment Weekly named The College Dropout the best album of the 2000s, while Complex named Graduation the best album released between 2002 and 2012.

Beyond music, West expanded into fashion and business. He collaborated with Nike, Louis Vuitton, Gap, and Adidas. His Yeezy brand became highly influential in sneakers and streetwear. He also founded DONDA, a creative company involved in design and media.

West expressed interest in architecture and launched a Yeezy architecture initiative focused partly on social housing concepts and prefabricated homes.

In August 2021, he filed to legally change his name from “Kanye Omari West” to simply “Ye,” saying the change was for personal reasons. The request was approved in October 2021. He had previously explained that “ye” was one of the most commonly used words in the Bible and represented “you,” “us,” and humanity collectively. He also referred to “Kanye West” as his “slave name.”

West was married to Kim Kardashian from 2014 to 2022. They have four children: North, Saint, Chicago, and Psalm. Their relationship was one of the most heavily covered celebrity relationships in modern media.

West has frequently been involved in controversies throughout his career. He has had public feuds with artists including Taylor Swift and Drake. His interruption of Taylor Swift at the 2009 MTV Video Music Awards became one of the most famous moments in pop culture history.

In 2016, West canceled part of the Saint Pablo Tour and was hospitalized after reports of exhaustion, sleep deprivation, dehydration, paranoia, and mental health concerns.

West publicly discussed struggles involving mental health, addiction, alcohol use, and opioid dependency after liposuction surgery. Multiple reports and interviews discussed his bipolar disorder diagnosis, though he later questioned aspects of the diagnosis.

In 2022, West faced major backlash over antisemitic statements and social media posts. Companies including Adidas ended partnerships with him, causing Forbes to report that he was no longer a billionaire.

In later years, reports described multiple public apologies and attempts at reconciliation with Jewish communities.

West has also been involved in politics and ran as an independent candidate in the 2020 United States presidential election. He later remained politically active and controversial through social media and interviews.
